Occupational Hazards


1.) Slips, trips and falls: Slips may occur on oily floors and when one works with slippery parts of vehicles. Also there are chances of tripping over tools and equipment.

2.) Exposure to noise, vibrations, fire and other hazardous conditions: Noise and vibrations can result in deafening of ears. Workers are also exposed to hazardous substances and chemicals- petrol, solvents, degreasers, paints and other cleaning products.

3.) Manual handling: Worker actions of bending, reaching, stretching, pulling, lifting, working in cramped positions and repetitive motions. If a worker bends over an engine bay, he/she may hold a fixed posture for a long period of time. Using excessive force with hand tools can also cause injuries.

4.) Being hit by or trapped between objects: During grinding, working under vehicles and cleaning parts, particles emitted may affect the eyes and other body parts. Doors, bonnets and other parts, tools and equipment may slip or move while performing work.

Occupational Hazards

1.) Dust inhalation or coal dust is one of the most common concerns for miners.

2.) Mines are noisy places, with the constant of drilling and heavy machinery, and the potential for hearing damage is quite serious.

3.) Whole body vibration (WBV) is a slow forming physical hazard that occurs in mining workers and other occupations that work with heavy machinery.

4.) For open-pit miners, understanding the risk of over-exposure to UV (ultraviolet) radiation in sunlight is essential.

5.) Musculoskeletal disorders (MSDs) refer to any problems affecting your bones, muscles, blood vessels and nerves.
